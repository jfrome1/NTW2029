## Purpose

This exercise helps you consolidate what you learned during our conference about the disagreement you're analyzing. By articulating the key insights from our discussion, you'll clarify your understanding and identify what you still need to investigate before writing your draft.

## Overview

Answer the following questions about the conference discussion and your analytical next steps.

## Requirements

Copy and paste the questions below into a document and type your answer below each question, in full sentences. Keep the questions numbered:

1. In P06, you aimed to verify how the commentary characterizes the target. Did the conference change your view of this characterization? Explain either way. (It's ok if it didn't.)
2. Did the conference change anything about your understanding of the disagreement between the target and the commentary during the conference? If so, what changed and why?
3. What specific research or analysis do you plan to do based on the conference? This might include things such as looking up sources cited by the target or commentary articles, checking specific passages more carefully, investigating whether a concept means the same thing to both authors, or other activities.
4. Of the things you're still uncertain about, what do you think is the most important to gain clarity on, and why?
5. (optional) Do you have any other comments or feedback?

Include a GenAI link/non-use statement (required) but not a word count.

## Guidelines

It's normal to leave the conference without a thesis. If you don't yet know what your thesis will be, that's expected--your thesis should emerge from the analysis you do next, not before it.
