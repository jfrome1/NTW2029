## What the data is good for

### 1) Navigation and content pathways
- $pageview, internalLinkClick (link and text), and externalLinkClick together gives us a near-complete map of how students move through the site. You can reconstruct per-student reading paths.

### 2) Content Engagement Depth
- The "read heartbeat event" fires 15 seconds of active reading/scrolling, tagged with a "page" property. This gives us a proxy for time-on-page per student per URL without relying on $pageleave. It would be our richest engagement signal.

### 3) Nutshell Feature Engagement
- The Nutshell UI (expandable content summaries) has strong open volume (5,220) and a meaningful "inactiveNutshell" count (3439 - fired when nutshell goes idle). This is a useable measure of deeper content curiosity.

### 4) Attention State
- tabFocused / tabUnfocused (collected since May 2026? technically we don't have the data then.) : it gives us the timeFocused, timeHidden, and totalTimeOpen per page visit. So, this would be a more precise measurement method than the heartbeat feature alone.



1) read (7,589 events) : our proxy for time-on-page.
2) $pageview + internalLinkClick : navigation pathways
3) openNutshell / inactiveNutshell : content curiosity
