# Handbook of Learning Analytics : Introduction

<h3>Some Pre-requisites</h3>

There are 4 ways to understand Learning Analytics (LA)

1) LA as a concern or problem to be solved
2) LA as an opportunity
3) LA as a field of inquiry
4) The researchers and practitioners that make up the LA community


There are wide variety of interpretations when thinking about LA. Its literal deifinition is "analytics of learning".

"Learning" is like a universal experience, so it is open to interpretation by anyone, including a range of academic fields.
- I think this is basically saying that LA can be learned by anyone who's interested in this field.

The distinction between "analytics" and "analysis" is vague even among professionals
- It makes sense since during the 20th century, "analytics" was for pure mathematics, when they dealt with prime numbers.

So, it seems like the area of applied data analysis really is a recent thing



<h3>The "Concern" of Learning Analytics</h3>

- LA has been concerned with solving the problems associated with growth in the availability, quantity, speed, and type of data in learning environments.

- Learning & Knowledge Conference (2011) : Growth of data is faster than organizations trying to make sense of it. This is a concern.
- These problems are said to be mainly technical. But, nowadays, progress has been made such that researchers know how to effectively deal with standards, technical architectures, and the educational technology landscape especially in institutions of higher education.

- But, as of 2022, collecting, managing, and analyzing massive amounts of student data became doable due to technology.

- However, institional adoption pose a substantial hurdle to widespread use of LA and there aren't enough cases where large-scale implementations in which LA became a tool to solve educational problems. (I thought this was solved? Why?)

- It tells us how despite we have certain technology to somehow solve the technical problems of LA, the concerns in the field still grew substantially.

- They suggest that the list of concerns are :
1) Privacy
2) Ethics
3) Data Ownership
4) Equity
5) Usability
6) State and Direction of LA itself


- Growth of the concerns, however, gave a greater sense of clarity around specific problems adjacent to data.
- Basically saying that the growing amount of concerns let us understand what the problems are in the domain of data analysis

- The real question is : "How do we collect more useful data?"

<br>

- so, when considering what LA is, we can point to an ever growing list of concerns that emanate from educational data and the technology that facilitate their collection.

- So, making send of accumulated data is still identified as one of the important concerns despite all technological developments.
- LA is all about solving long standing problems in education and creating new opportunities in education

- I guess what this is saying is that the main objective of LA is to find a problem in an educational setting, so this could be the NTW mod, or any other modules or classes that provides educaiton. Then, we analyze and create solutions to those specific problems using LA.





 <h3>The "Opportunity" of Learning Analytics</h3>

- Advancement in computing technologies is one of the many advantages LA can get as an opportunity.
- So, technology can mirror the promises such as efficienty and reducing work
- Using these LA can remake education.

- Evolution of LA as an opportunity actually came from business analytics (BA).
- In 1970, BA started to be a boom when it helped businesses to gain insight from data, and making decision making easier.

- Well, as predicted, BA became a good tool for businesses to be profitable

- BA affected LA in various fields in universities, through the adoption of ideas, practices and tools, and Human Resources department.
- The logical question asked is : "What might translate between the management of resources and the management of learning?"

- 1980~1990 : Started using data to find actionable insights to make better business decisions
- Shift to student operations : Now, registrar's offices use this. Specific data such as student records and class schedules were analyzed.

- 2000s : "Academic Analysis" rose (this is an actual name of the formal field) where instead of just tracking data, institutions built advanced models to forecast the future. They can understand the enrollment trends, student retention rates, and early warning signs for student failure


- So, we can realize the missed opportunities or prevent institutions from missing important details for learning, supporting students' education. 


- In the US, 15 years of "No Child Left Behind" legislation emphasized the connection between student progress and robust data systems.
- It is important to think about whether analytic sysmtems produce improvements in student learning, but one verified impact that remains the backbone of the analytics enterprise is the Learning Management System (LMS)


<h3>Moving onto Learning Management Systems</h3>

- LMSs expanded the data pool beyond administrative activities to actions directly taken by students while learning. (Our case)
- This overcomes the barrier that might have prevented data collection and combination.
- These systems took time to develop whil Content Management Systems (CMS) and Software as a Service (SAAS) had big effect.


- Platforms like Moddle opened doors to extensive, organized and centralized data streams that couls be utilized to investigate learning.
- So, early adopters in the institutions of higher edcuation began to see the possibilities of observing patterns in student data.
- The idea of these data sources is that there lies uncovered insight into learning

- Note that unlike analyzing profit, revenues in BA, learning is a more slippery construct.
