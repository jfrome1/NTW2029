# Online Learning Strategy Transition in Blended Courses: Learning Analytics Based on Two Cases in Higher Education

<h3>Research Objective & Problem Statement</h3>

- As we know, Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) can be traced and analyzed, but it relies on "discrete" values such as total page visits, number of times the user clicked a button, etc. The authors for this paper suggests, we need to focus in a "continuous" way.

- Until now, we have been reading research papers that has been suggesting how understanding students' dynamic behaviors are more important. So, we need to know "why" students transitioned from a specific state to another.

- Hence, we need to understand the sequential nature of SRL and oversimplify the overall learning process

- <b>Objective : </b>Integrating SRL theory into every step of the data analysis pipeline to identify common learning sequences from trace data. Then, the researchers sought these sequences into clusters (just like the previous paper), and link it to the academic achievements. This would tell us which clusters (which specific traces) led the students to perform better.
