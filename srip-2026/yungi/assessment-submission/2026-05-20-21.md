# Theory-Informed Sequence Analyses to Investigate self-regulated Learning Processes and their link to Achievement

<h3>Research Objective & Problem Statement</h3>

- Problem : Understanding the temporal dynamics of Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) and its link to achievement remains unexplored.
  
- So, while other papers focus on aggregated behavioral indicators (total clicks, page views), this research focuses on the sequential, dynamic nature of how learning unfolds.

- The objective : The study aimed to integrate robust theoretical SRL models into every step of the data analysis pipeline. By doing so, they sought to identify common sequences across learners as indicators of SRL, cluster learners based on these behaviors, and link those clusters to both academic achievement and individual differences like motivation.


- Research Question : The study investigated what theory-based analysis reveals about student learning, what learner groups emerge from data-driven clustering, how these groups relate to achievement, and whether they differ in individual characteristics (motivation, prior knowledge)
