<h3>Research Objective & Problem Statement</h3>

- Problem : Understanding the temporal dynamics of Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) and its link to achievement remains unexplored.
  
- So, while other papers focus on aggregated behavioral indicators (total clicks, page views), this research focuses on the sequential, dynamic nature of how learning unfolds.

- The objective : The study aimed to integrate robust theoretical SRL models into every step of the data analysis pipeline. By doing so, they sought to identify common sequences across learners as indicators of SRL, cluster learners based on these behaviors, and link those clusters to both academic achievement and individual differences like motivation.


- Research Question : The study investigated what theory-based analysis reveals about student learning, what learner groups emerge from data-driven clustering, how these groups relate to achievement, and whether they differ in individual characteristics (motivation, prior knowledge)


<h3>Methodology : kind of different from ours</h3>

- The targeted audience was 448 7th grade students using an Intelligent Tutoring System
- SRL (self-regulated learning) = how a student manages their own brain and behavior while they study. They don't just absorb information, but they use three step loops : forethough(planning), performance(doing & monitoring), and appraisal (reflecting)
- So, when we are studying students' behavioral data, we mostly think of the "PERFORMANCE" phase, trying to figure out what students do when they are stuck.
  
1) Data Preprocessing : They filtered the raw log data to include only relevant, learner-initiated interactions and augmented it with context (whether correct, or if feedback was provided) (?)

2) Sequence Extraction : Instead of calculating Markov probabilities, they use the TraMineR algorithm to mine the data for recurring, temporally ordered patterns composed of at least three events

3) SRL Labelling : They applied a deductive-inductive approach to manually label the extracted sequences with psychological SRL processess (forethought, performance, appraisal)

4) Learner Clustering : They calculated the proportional frequency of each SRL label for every student and used k-means clustering to group students with similar behavioral profiles.

5) Achievement & Individual Differences Analysis : Finally, they used Kruskal-Wallis test to compare the resulting clusters against the students' post-test scores and survey data on motivation and prior knowledge.
