# Learning Analytics in Online Learning Environment: A Systematic Review on the Focuses and the Types of Student-Related Analytics Data

- This paper basically provides a systematic review of the application of Learning Analytics (LA) in online learning environments.
- It talks about how LA can be used to enhance the quality of teaching and learning materials

<h3>Research Objectives</h3>

- The researchers establish a threefold objective to understand the current landscape of LA in online learning. Their goals were :

1) Ascertain the primary focuses of learning analytics research in online environments and examine the significant results.

2) Identify the types of student-related analytics data being collected, alongside any assiciated issues with that data.

3) Determine the extent to which learning anlaytics interventions have been practically applied in these environments.


<h3>Methodologies</h3>

- The authors conducted a systematic literature review to identify relevant empirical studies.

1) Framework : The review strictly adhered to the four procedures outlined by the PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) guidelines: Identifiction, Screening, Eligibility, and Included.

2) Data Collection : The researchers searched multiple major academic databases, including the ACM Digital Library, LearnTechLib, ERIC, ScienceDirect, Scopus, Web of Science, and SpringerLink. The search was restricted to articles published between 2012~2020.

3) Selection & Classification : Out of an initial 1198 articles, 34 were ultimately selected for deep analysis after rigorous screening. These articles were then classified using two established frameworks: the Learning Analytics Reference Model (categorizing the focus of the research) and the Learners'Learning Status Data framework (to categorize the types of data used).


<h3>Main Points and Findings</h3>

- The majority of LA research heavily prioritizes monitoring analysis (48.33%) and prediction/intervention (35.00%).

- A common task within monitoring and analysis is tracking students' online activities to produce reports that help teachers and institutions make informed decisions.
