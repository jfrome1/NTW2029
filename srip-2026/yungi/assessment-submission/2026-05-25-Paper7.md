# Learning Analytics in Online Learning Environment: A Systematic Review on the Focuses and the Types of Student-Related Analytics Data

- This paper basically provides a systematic review of the application of Learning Analytics (LA) in online learning environments.
- It talks about how LA can be used to enhance the quality of teaching and learning materials

<h3>Research Objectives</h3>

- The researchers establish a threefold objective to understand the current landscape of LA in online learning. Their goals were :

1) Ascertain the primary focuses of learning analytics research in online environments and examine the significant results.

2) Identify the types of student-related analytics data being collected, alongside any assiciated issues with that data.

3) Determine the extent to which learning anlaytics interventions have been practically applied in these environments.


<h3>Methodologies</h3>

- The authors conducted a systematic literature review to identify relevant empirical studies.

1) Framework : The review strictly adhered to the four procedures outlined by the PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) guidelines: Identifiction, Screening, Eligibility, and Included.

2) Data Collection : The researchers searched multiple major academic databases, including the ACM Digital Library, LearnTechLib, ERIC, ScienceDirect, Scopus, Web of Science, and SpringerLink. The search was restricted to articles published between 2012~2020.

3) Selection & Classification : Out of an initial 1198 articles, 34 were ultimately selected for deep analysis after rigorous screening. These articles were then classified using two established frameworks: the Learning Analytics Reference Model (categorizing the focus of the research) and the Learners'Learning Status Data framework (to categorize the types of data used).




<h3>Main Points and Findings</h3>

1) Primary focuses of Learning Analytics

- The majority of LA research heavily prioritizes monitoring analysis (48.33%) and prediction/intervention (35.00%).

- A common task within monitoring and analysis is tracking students' online activities to produce reports that help teachers and institutions make informed decisions. (I guess this would mostly be our case too)

- Conversely, there is a notable lack of resaerch focused on other areas of the Learning Analytics Reference Model, such as assessment / feedback (10%), reflection (3.33%), adaption (1.67%), and personalization / recommendation (1.67%)



2) Types of Student-Related Data Collected

- Learning Behavior Data : This is the most widely investigated data type (37.04%). It includes quantifiable metrics like login times, study time, and click rates. While it is easy to gather these data using LMS, it only provides surface-level inspection of student engagement.

- Learning Level Data : The second most common data type (30.86%), which encompasses exam grades and direct program scores. This is frequently used because it is a fast indicator of overall learning performance.

- Learning Emotional Data : This is the "least" examined data type (7.41%). Emotional data involves psychological factors like feelings, motivation, and anxiety. The authors note that this data is dfficult to precisely interpret due to its abstract nature, yet it is often highly corelated with student dropout rates.

- Data Issues : The authors highlight several ongoing challenges with analytics data, including difficulties in managing massive datasets, ensuring LA results are disseminated to all stakeholders (students, faculties, institutions) rather than just teachers, and navigating complex ethical concerns regarding data ownership and informed consent.



3) The State of LA interventions

- Practical, applied interventions are still in a developmental infancy.
- Among the 34 articles reviewed, only a few specifically focused on the intervention aspect
- Currently, the trend in LA intervention leans heavily toward theoretical construction, such as designing models or frameworks to guide future execution, rather than executing active interventions that directly influence student activity. (I guess machine learning algorithms can be said that it is "theoretical")

- Hence, if we were to construct a theory, we need to find a way to actually apply it so that it influences our students' activities.


<h3>Conclusion</h3>

- Current application of learning analytics in online environments is predominantly oriented toward monitoring, analysis, and prediction, utilizing easily accessible behavior and performance-level data. However, to maximize the potential of LA, we need to do something other than just collecting and visulizing data. We need to make sense of it.

- For future studies, the authors strongly recommend that reserachers focus on the under-investigated "psychological and emotional" aspects of students.

- Furthermore, they call for the development of more concrete LA interventions and clear guidelines to help both teachers and studetns effectively interpret complex algorithmic data, which will ultimately enhance the overall success of teaching and learning
