## Major Inquiries across Educational Research in the early 21st Century

1) What constitutes improvement?"
2) What should the relationship between data and instructor be?
3) How can the implementation of LA be done responsibly?
4) What role does the student play in the system?
5) And in what way will analytics aid in the development of Artificial Intelligence and vice versa?

<br>


<h3>A field of Inquiry</h3>

- Definition of "Inquiry" : Systematic Investigation, a study, or a line of intellectual questioning. So, basically when academics explore problems and try to find answers, they are engaging in inquiry.

- Important to ask : "What makes LA a field of inquiry in its own right?" I think this means what makes LA so attractive to researchers that it is reserach-able?
- What makes LA a "field" of educational research? What make LA be distinguished from other fields?

- The shared concepts of LA that holds the field together, is dependent on how we define the field itself. 

<br>

<h3>LA is a "human in the loop" argument</h3>

- although automation is powerful, education as a social enterprise requires human decision making.
- this has spawned inquiry into where and how humans and machines should interact in the processing and consumption of educational data through data collection, algorithms, dashboards, alerts, simulations, and/or policies
- this "human in the loop" in LA creates a level of internal conssitency.

- Hence, a <b>successful line of inquiry</b> within LA can be defined as one in which data is utilized to investigate the partnership between machine and humans in the learning process

- So, a "Progress" is made when a greater understanding of these interactions is found, or applied in ways that facilitate the process (the process they are talking about here, is I am assuming students' educational process and actions)

- Of course, these researches would be different from those where they say that teachers, or educators should be replaced by machines

<br>


<h3>Charaterizing the scope of the field using <b>Bibliometrics</b></h3>

- Common themes in research papers that contain "learning analytics" were technology, data, and education. Beyond these, there are higher education, big data, machine learning, data mining, and prediction. (And I also saw learning analytics using leading Eigenvalues-based clustering - everything is a matrix!!)

- MOOC (Massive Open Online Course), LMS also were highly correlated to the theme of learning analytics
- There are also things like visualization, collaboration, and assessment.

- This may seem like LA is distinguished from other fields, but there are related fields that would likely claim to share the same concerns and see the the same opportunities in the growth of technology-mediated data in education. These would be data mining, AI in education, learning sciences, computer supported collaborative learning, and the more recent educational data and learning engineering.


<h3>Methodology in LA</h3>

- Researchers suggest that methodology within LA is far ranging and there is no truly common language or processes across the field by which researchers demonstrate evidence.
